NEGRO SLAVERY.

Petitions, praying for the abolition of negro slavery, were
presented by Mr. Byng from a town in Perthshire; Sir W.
Foulkes, from the inhabitants of Wells, Norfolk; Mr. B.
Carter, from the inhabitants of Portsmouth; and -by Mr. Mar-
shall, from the clergy, bankers, and inhabitants of Leeds,
signed by 18,800 persons.
